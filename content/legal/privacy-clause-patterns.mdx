# what we save from contract scans

when you scan a brand deal contract, you can optionally check a box to help improve the tool. here's exactly what that means.

## what we do NOT save

- your contract text. it's kept for 7 days (so your shareable result link works), then permanently deleted.
- your name, your brand's name, or any party names.
- dollar amounts.
- dates.
- email addresses, phone numbers, or tax IDs — those are stripped before the AI even sees the contract.

## what we DO save (if you opt in)

the structural pattern of each flagged clause — what type of clause it is and roughly what it says, with all identifying info removed.

example: a clause like

> "Creator John Doe grants Acme Brand a perpetual worldwide license to use all video content for $5,000 paid Net 60."

becomes:

> "Creator grants perpetual worldwide license to all content for paid and organic use across platforms with Net 60 payment."

no names. no dollar amounts. just the structure.

we also save:

- your content niche (gaming, beauty, finance, etc.) — only if you selected one
- the platform the deal was for (inferred from your context note)
- rough audience tier and deal size tier — not exact numbers, just buckets like "10k-100k" or "1k-5k"

## why we save it

brand deal contracts are notoriously opaque. brands know what every creator signs. creators don't know what's normal.

the clause pattern database is how we change that. as the dataset grows, we'll be able to tell you things like: "73% of YouTube gaming deals include a 90-day exclusivity window" or "kill fees under 25% are rare in beauty deals over $5k."

that's the kind of information that's worth real money to a creator negotiating their first big deal.

## who can see it

right now: us. we review patterns manually before building any public-facing statistics.

eventually: aggregated, anonymized benchmarks surface in the scanner results — "this exclusivity clause is longer than usual for your niche." raw patterns are never published. only statistics.

## how to opt out after the fact

email us at privacy@thecfoforcreators.com with your scan date and niche. we'll delete any patterns associated with your scan.

your contract text is already gone after 7 days regardless.
