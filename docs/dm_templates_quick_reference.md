# DM Templates — Quick Reference (keep this open while sending)

Voice: lowercase, peer-to-peer, "i built it because [creator pain], sharing in case useful." No introductions. No paid product mention. No asks. 60–110 words.

Per DM workflow:
1. Click the creator's profile URL from the Sheet
2. Check their audience size (10k–100k = send, else skip)
3. Look at their 2-3 most recent posts/videos
4. Pick the matched template letter from the Sheet
5. Replace `[name]` and `[one-sentence specific reference]` with what you actually saw
6. Send via CreatorLand inbox
7. Mark `sent` = yes in the Sheet

## Personalization line examples (the [one-sentence specific reference])

**Good** (specific, shows you actually looked):
- "your recent video on q3 estimateds caught me"
- "saw your post about brand deal frustrations last week"
- "the breakdown you did on multi-platform creator income made sense"
- "your collab with [brand] was a smart fit for your audience"
- "your masterclass on creator business felt sharp"
- "caught your podcast episode on the [topic]"

**Bad** (skip these — sounds like marketing):
- "love your content!" — Never. This is the universal "I'm about to pitch you" signal
- "huge fan of your work" — Same
- "i really admire how you" — Same
- Generic compliments without referencing specific content
- Anything that doesn't prove you actually looked at their recent stuff

## Quick "is this DM ready to send?" check

Before hitting send on each DM, ask:
1. Does the first line reference SOMETHING specific I actually saw? If no → don't send.
2. Is the body word-for-word from a template? If yes → good (your personalization is in the opener).
3. Did I add any asks ("would love to hop on a call", "let me know what you think")? If yes → delete them.
4. Did I sign with anything other than "— jada"? If yes → change to "— jada".
5. Is the total DM under 110 words? Count them. Anything longer reads as a pitch.

If all 5 pass: send.
