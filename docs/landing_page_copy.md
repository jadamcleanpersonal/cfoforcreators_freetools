It's not a bank. It's not a CPA. It's not a budgeting app. It's the financial operator that sits between you and the real professionals — making sure they have what they need and making sure you know what's actually going on.

# Design notes for whoever's building this

- **Mobile-first.** Most creators will see this on a phone. Big tap targets, scrollable single column, the email field always reachable.
- **The counter is the visual hook.** Make it prominent. Real-time tick down as people sign up creates urgency. Could even animate it briefly when someone new joins.
- **Spots-remaining is the conversion lever.** Show it in three places: hero, after the offer block, sticky footer. Every time someone scrolls past it, urgency builds.
- **Founder section needs a real photo + handwritten signature feel.** Builds trust. Generic stock = death.
- **The email field needs to actually validate** (not just store junk). Bonus: ask for a name on submit so emails feel personal, not bulk.
- **No video, no scroll-jacking, no live chat widget.** Keep it fast and quiet. The product itself is the show.
- **Prepare 3 variants** for A/B testing later: one with the counter, one without, one with social proof ("47 creators joined this week"). Start with the counter version — it's the strongest hypothesis.

# Voice / brand notes

- Use lowercase headlines if it fits the brand (creator-native, less corporate).
- "We" is fine but "I" (founder voice) is stronger in the founder section.
- Never use "consult a professional" — it's a phrase the AI CFO is built to NOT say. Same on the landing page.
- Don't oversell. Honest framing wins this audience. "We're building," "coming soon," "join the waitlist" — not "the future of creator finance is here."
